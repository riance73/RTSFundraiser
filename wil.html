Who am I?

My name is Wil and I am a resident of Boston, Massachusetts. I am a senior at Charlestown high and recently enrolled myself into the RTS program. Last year I had attended an online school due to the fact that my family had to move artound alot. But the online school did not provide the credits needed to complete my Junior year. When I learned they wanted me to retake the year I flat out refused and instead I decided to take charge of my future. And in searching for solutions I came across the RTS program.

What does RTS mean to me?

RTS is an opportunity for me to gain back credits that I had missed during my previous high school experience. Honestly, I had lost all motivation to finish my school work. RTS has been extremely supportive of me and has put me back on track to graduating on time. I will graduate this year. I have also made many new friends and connections through this program. They don't only help with gaining credits needed for graduation, they also provide insight on the future after highschool.

Why does this project matter?

This project is a good way to connect with classmates and teachers and to learn how to create a space for everyone to feel comfortable. When I first attended RTS when I walked into the space I felt secluded from everyone else. Everyone seemed to be so apart from each other and with the dim lighting it just felt very unwelcoming. For the first few weeks I would sit far apart from everyone in the program which prevented me from making connections with others. With this project, we can make RTS a far more welcoming place for future RTS students. By making a donation today, you can help contribute to making this project a reality.
